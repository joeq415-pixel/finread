      What These Metrics Mean

      Return on Assets (ROA)

      How much profit the company makes per dollar of assets it owns.

      Return on Equity (ROE)

      How much profit the company makes on shareholder investments.

      FCF Conversion Rate

      What percentage of profits turn into actual cash.

      Debt/Equity Ratio

      How much the company borrows compared to what shareholders own.

      Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)

      How efficiently the company uses both debt and equity to make profits.

      Interest Coverage

      How many times the company can pay its interest obligations with its operating income. Higher is better.

      Debt to Free Cash Flow

      How many years it would take to pay off debt using free cash flow. Lower is better.
